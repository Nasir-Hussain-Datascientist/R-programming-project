Introduction

In this report, we analyze traffic accident data collected from the Addis Ababa Sub-city police departments. The dataset spans from 2017 to 2020 and includes various features such as driver demographics, vehicle information, accident conditions, and accident severity. This analysis aims to uncover patterns and key factors influencing traffic accident severity in Addis Ababa.

Objective

This project seeks to answer the following research questions:

  1. What are the primary causes of road traffic accidents in Addis Ababa?

  2. How do environmental and temporal factors (e.g., weather, time of day) influence accident severity?

  3. What patterns can we observe based on driver demographics and vehicle information?

  4. Is there a correlation between vehicle-related factors (e.g., type of vehicle, service year) and accident severity?

  5. Are there time-based trends in accident occurrence, such as peaks during certain times of the day or days of the week?

  6. How do different road features (e.g., road alignment, type of junction) affect accident frequency and severity?

  7. Is there a relationship between the number of vehicles involved in an accident and the severity of the outcome?

  8. How does pedestrian movement influence accident outcomes?

  9. Does the driving experience of the driver affect the likelihood of severe accidents?

  10. Can we predict accident severity based on the drivers and vehicles features?
